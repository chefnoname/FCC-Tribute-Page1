Masjid Quba

Official Wikipedia page

The Quba Mosque (Arabic: مَسْجِد قُبَاء) is a mosque located on the outskirts of Medina, Saudi Arabia. Initially, the mosque was built 6 kilometres (3.7 miles) off Medina in the village of Quba, before Medina expanded to include this village.

According to records, its first stones were positioned by Muhammad ﷺ as soon as he arrived on his emigration from the city of Mecca to Medina,[5] and the mosque was completed by his companions. Muhammad ﷺ spent 14 days in this mosque praying qaṣr (Arabic: قَـصْـر‎, a short prayer) while waiting for Ali to arrive in Medina.

The latter stayed behind in Mecca to safeguard Muhammad’s ﷺ life and safe escape by sleeping in Muhammad’s ﷺ bed in his place, an event referred to in the Quran, Sura al-Baqara verse 207. Also going along with traditional saying, this mosque is said to be where the first Friday prayer was held, led by Muhammad ﷺ.
